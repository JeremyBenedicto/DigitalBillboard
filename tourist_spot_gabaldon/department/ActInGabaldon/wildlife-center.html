Where Wildlife Gets a Second Chance

At Calabasa Wildlife Rehabilitation Center, we provide sanctuary, healing, and hope to rescued wildlife. Our dedicated team works tirelessly to rehabilitate injured and displaced animals, offering visitors a unique opportunity to witness conservation efforts firsthand and connect with the incredible creatures that call the Philippines home.

Kiko

Philippine Monkey (Macaca fascicularis philippensis)

Highly intelligent and social animals, the Philippine Monkey, a subspecies of the Long-tailed Macaque, is a common sight in the Philippines. They live in large groups and have a complex social structure.

Lawin

Philippine Serpent Eagle (Spilornis holospilus)

A common raptor found throughout the Philippines, the Serpent Eagle is a skilled hunter, primarily feeding on snakes and lizards. They are often seen soaring over forests and open country.

Kuwago

Philippine Eagle-Owl

One of the largest owl species in the Philippines, the Eagle-Owl is a formidable nocturnal hunter. Its deep hooting calls are a distinctive sound of the Philippine night.

Kalaw

Philippine Hornbill(Buceros hydrocorax)

Known as the 'farmer of the forest,' the Philippine Hornbill is vital for seed dispersal, helping to maintain the health of the forest. Their large, colorful casque is a distinctive feature.

Agila

Philippine Hawk-Eagle (Nisaetus philippensis)

The Philippine Hawk-Eagle is a powerful bird of prey endemic to the Philippines. It is a symbol of the country's rich biodiversity and the wild spirit of its forests.
